## Overview

Vitu's Exercise feature automatically tracks your physical activity throughout the day using your device's sensors and GPS. It counts your steps, detects whether you're walking or running, and intelligently **ignores step counts when you're in a vehicle** traveling faster than 15 km/h.

## How to Use

<Steps>
  <Step title="Grant Permissions">
    When you first open the Exercise screen, Vitu will request:
    - **Location services**: To measure your speed and detect vehicle movement
    - **Motion sensors**: To count steps from accelerometer data
    
    Accept these permissions for the feature to work properly.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Keep App Open (or Running in Background)">
    The app actively monitors your movement while on the Exercise screen. Your step count updates in real-time as you move.
    
    <Note>
      For continuous tracking throughout the day, keep the app running in the background or return to the Exercise tab periodically to sync your count.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Monitor Your Activity">
    The screen displays:
    - Current step count for today
    - Progress percentage toward your 10,000-step daily goal
    - Current activity type (walking, running, stationary, or in vehicle)
    - Current speed in km/h
    - Real-time cardio chart showing step bursts every 10 seconds
  </Step>

  <Step title="Pause When Needed">
    Tap the **"Pausar"** button to temporarily stop counting steps. This is useful if you're moving around but don't want to count it (e.g., pacing while on a phone call).
    
    Tap **"Reanudar"** to continue tracking.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Data is Tracked

### Daily Metrics

- **Steps**: Total number of steps detected today
- **Speed**: Current movement speed in kilometers per hour
- **Activity Type**: 
  - **Caminando** (Walking): Speed < 6 km/h with movement
  - **Corriendo** (Running): Speed 6-12 km/h with strong movement
  - **Parado** (Stationary): Speed < 0.5 km/h
  - **En vehículo** (In Vehicle): Speed > 15 km/h

<Info>
  When you're detected as "En vehículo," step counting is **automatically paused** to prevent false counts from bumpy roads or vibrations.
</Info>

### Historical Data

Vitu stores your step count for the **past 7 days**, allowing you to see your weekly activity trends.

## How Results Are Displayed

### Main Progress Circle

At the top of the screen, a large circular progress indicator shows:
- **Percentage completed** of your 10,000-step daily goal
- **Total steps today** in large text
- Color-coded by your theme's seed color

### Activity Status Bar

Below the main circle, you'll see:
- An **icon** representing your current activity (walking, running, vehicle, or stationary)
- Your **activity label** in Spanish
- **Current speed** in km/h
- **Estimated calories** burned (calculated as steps × 0.04)

### Real-Time Cardio Chart

The app displays a **line chart** that updates every 10 seconds, showing:
- Number of steps taken in each 10-second interval
- Up to the last 10 minutes of data (60 data points maximum)
- Smooth, curved line with shaded area underneath

This chart helps you see your activity intensity in real-time.

### Weekly Bar Chart

Scroll down to see a bar chart showing your daily step counts for the **past 7 days** (Monday through Sunday):
- Each bar represents one day
- Height corresponds to total steps that day (max scale: 12,000 steps)
- Days are labeled: L, M, X, J, V, S, D

## Tips for Best Results

### Allow Background Location

For the most accurate vehicle detection:
- Grant "Allow all the time" or "Allow while using the app" location permission
- Enable high-accuracy GPS in your device settings

### Keep Your Phone on You

The accelerometer detects steps based on your phone's movement, so:
- Carry your phone in your pocket, bag, or use an armband
- Holding it in your hand works, but pocket placement is more accurate

### Understand Step Sensitivity

Vitu uses different thresholds for step detection:
- **Walking threshold**: Accelerometer magnitude > 0.9
- **Running threshold**: Accelerometer magnitude > 1.4
- **Debounce timing**: 320-450 milliseconds between steps (to avoid double-counting)

<Note>
  Steps are saved to local storage every **10 steps**, so even if the app crashes, you won't lose much progress.
</Note>

### Daily Reset

Your step count automatically resets to zero at midnight each day. Previous days' data is preserved in the weekly chart.

## How Vehicle Detection Works

Vitu's smart vehicle detection prevents false step counts:

1. **GPS Speed Monitoring**: The app continuously checks your movement speed
2. **15 km/h Threshold**: If speed exceeds 15 km/h, you're marked as "En vehículo"
3. **Step Blocking**: While in vehicle mode, accelerometer events are ignored
4. **Automatic Resume**: When speed drops below 15 km/h, step counting resumes

<Warning>
  If you're cycling or on a fast electric scooter (>15 km/h), steps won't be counted. This is intentional to prevent false positives.
</Warning>

## Understanding Your Activity Types

### Stationary (Parado)
- Speed < 0.5 km/h
- You're standing still or sitting
- Icon: Meditation pose

### Walking (Caminando)
- Speed: 0.5-6 km/h
- Moderate movement detected
- Icon: Walking person

### Running (Corriendo)
- Speed: 6-12 km/h
- Strong movement with high accelerometer magnitude (>1.4)
- Icon: Running person

### In Vehicle (En vehículo)
- Speed > 15 km/h
- Steps are **not counted**
- Icon: Car
- Status shows in gray to indicate paused counting

## Troubleshooting

### "Activa el servicio de ubicación"
Your device's location services are turned off. Go to Settings > Location and enable it.

### "Permiso de ubicación"
You denied location permission. Go to Settings > Apps > Vitu > Permissions and grant location access.

### Steps Not Counting
- Ensure you're moving with your phone
- Check that the app isn't paused (look for "Pausar" button, not "Reanudar")
- Verify you're not marked as "En vehículo" (which blocks counting)

### Steps Counting While Driving
If steps are being counted in a car:
- Ensure location permission is granted
- Check that GPS is enabled and has a signal
- The 15 km/h threshold may not be reached in slow city traffic

### Weekly Chart Shows Zero
Data is stored locally. If you recently installed the app or cleared data, historical counts will be empty until you accumulate new daily records.
